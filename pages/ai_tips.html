Secrets to Instantly Up Your Prompt Game

Unlock better results from ChatGPT and other AI chatbots with these expert prompt engineering tips

There's bad prompts, and then there's good prompts

If you’ve ever typed a question into ChatGPT and thought, “Huh. That’s not exactly what I wanted…” don't worry, you’re not alone.

Below you will find some handy flashcards to learn about prompt engineering, the art of crafting prompts that get you the best results from AI chatbots like ChatGPT. Prompt engineering is the process of designing and refining prompts to elicit the most accurate, relevant, and useful responses from AI models.

Most people are only scratching the surface of what AI chatbots can really do. But as someone who prompts AI professionally, I can tell you the difference between a good result and a great one often comes down to the way you ask.

Below are some of the best-kept secrets that prompt engineers use to unlock more helpful, creative, and accurate responses from ChatGPT and other chatbots.

Prompt Engineering Best Practices

Master these techniques to get the most out of your AI interactions.

Assign a Role

Give the AI an identity (e.g., "You are a marketing strategist") to prime it for more confident, structured, and relevant responses.

Instead of: "Tell me about marketing."
Try: "You are a world-class marketing strategist. What are three clever ways a small business could grow on TikTok?"

Chain-of-Thought Prompting

For multi-step tasks or logic puzzles, instruct the AI to "think step-by-step" or break down the process.

Instead of: "What’s the answer to this math problem?"
Try: "Let’s solve this step by step: [math problem]"

Set Clear Constraints

Add rules, context, and desired output formats (e.g., length, tone, keywords) to keep the model focused.

Instead of: "I need three subject lines for a Mother’s Day sale"
Try: "Give me three subject lines for a Mother’s Day sale — keep them under 50 characters and use playful language."

Break Down Complex Tasks

Guide the AI step by step for more control and accuracy, especially for lengthy or multifaceted requests.

Example:
  1. "Summarize this blog post."
  2. "Now rewrite that summary in a more casual tone."
  3. "Now turn it into an Instagram caption."

Provide Examples (Few-Shot)

Give the model a sample tone, style, or structure to mimic, showing it exactly what you expect.

Example: "Write a product description like this one: ‘These boots are made for adventure. Built tough, but cozy enough for your lazy Sunday scrolls.’ Now write one for a travel backpack."

Iterate and Refine

Treat prompting like debugging code. Adjust your prompt based on the AI's response and rerun until you get what you want.

Don't settle for the first output. If it's not quite right, provide specific feedback: "Make it shorter," "Use simpler language," or "Focus on benefits."

Specify Tone/Style

Explicitly state the desired tone or style (e.g., warm, funny, professional, formal) to guide the AI's language.

Instead of: "Write a thank-you note."
Try: "Write a thank-you note that’s warm and funny, like something you’d text a close friend."

The “3-Word Rule” & Advanced Prompting Techniques

Explore powerful methods to get more precise and creative AI outputs.

1. The “3-Word Rule”

What it means:

Add a short 3-word directive to your prompt to instantly improve tone, voice, or depth.

Examples:
  • “Like a lawyer” → structured & precise
  • “Be a teacher” → clear & instructional
  • “Act like Hemingway” → bold & minimal
Starter Prompts:
  • “Explain inflation like a professor”
  • “Summarize this like a journalist”
  • “Write a poem like a songwriter”

2. Role Play Your Prompt

What it means:

Ask the AI to take on a persona to match your desired style or outcome.

Examples:
  • “You are a startup advisor”
  • “Act as a nutrition coach”
  • “Respond as a 12-year-old”
Why it works: It frames the response with the right expertise, tone, or perspective.

3. Give a Goal

What it means:

Tell the AI exactly what you want to achieve.

Example Prompts:
  • “Help me prepare for a job interview”
  • “Write this email to boost sales”
  • “Make this paragraph sound more friendly”
Bonus Tip: Include the audience and intent.

4. Show vs. Tell

What it means:

Show the AI what you want with examples—it learns fast from patterns.

How to Use It:
  • Provide sample outputs
  • Use “Do it like this...”
  • Compare A vs. B styles in your prompt
Example: “Rewrite this paragraph to sound like this example below...”

5. Iterate, Don’t Expect Perfection

What it means:

The first output is rarely the final one. Refine your prompt as you go.

How to Iterate:
  • “Make this more concise”
  • “Add humor to this”
  • “Use simpler words”
Tip: Treat AI like a creative partner—not a vending machine.

6. Include Context Up Front

What it means:

Start your prompt by giving background, so the AI knows what you're aiming for.

Examples:
  • “I’m building a website for mental health awareness. Write a mission statement.”
  • “For a college audience unfamiliar with blockchain…”
Tip: The more you feed in, the more focused and useful the output becomes.

7. Use Constraints to Focus Output

What it means:

Guide the AI by setting specific limits like word count, tone, or format.

Examples:
  • “In 100 words, explain quantum physics for beginners.”
  • “Use only bullet points.”
  • “Keep the tone casual and upbeat.”
Why it works: Constraints sharpen creativity and reduce generic fluff.

8. Ask for Multiple Options

What it means:

Prompt the AI to generate 2–3 versions so you can compare and choose.

Examples:
  • “Give me 3 headline options for a fitness blog.”
  • “List 2 variations of this cold email, one funny and one formal.”
Bonus Tip: Combine the best elements from each result.

9. Use Follow-Up Prompts to Refine

What it means:

Don’t restart—revise. Use the output as a base and prompt again to tweak it.

Examples:
  • “Can you make this more persuasive?”
  • “Now rewrite it at a 5th-grade reading level.”
  • “Add more emotion to this section.”
Why it matters: Follow-ups unlock higher-quality results without starting from scratch.

The Surprising Power of Politeness in Prompting AI

Beyond technical aspects, politeness can significantly enhance your AI interactions.

Introduction to Politeness in AI

When it comes to interacting with artificial intelligence, most people focus on the technical aspects—being specific, adding context, or using structured formats. But there’s another, often-overlooked technique that can make your AI conversations smoother, more helpful, and even more humanlike: basic politeness.

Yes, you read that right. Saying “please” and “thank you” to an AI might seem unnecessary—after all, it’s not conscious or emotionally aware. But in many cases, those simple words can actually improve the quality of the responses you get. Here’s why.

1. Politeness Guides Tone

AI language models are trained on massive amounts of text data, including conversations, articles, instructions, and human interactions. When you say “please,” the AI picks up on that as a signal for a certain tone—typically more helpful, less aggressive, and more service-oriented.

Without politeness: “Summarize this article.”
With politeness: “Could you please summarize this article for me?”

The second version doesn’t just sound better—it often gets you a more thorough and friendlier response. Why? Because you’re signaling that you want a helpful, service-minded reply, and the AI picks up on that nuance.

2. “Thank You” Signals Completion and Gratitude

While AI doesn’t feel appreciated, saying “thank you” helps signal the end of a task or query. This can subtly affect follow-up responses, encouraging the AI to stay within the same polite tone or avoid redundant explanations.

Even better, saying “thank you” models good behavior for any audience reading the exchange. In classrooms, client interactions, or team environments, it reinforces digital etiquette and mutual respect—even when the other party is artificial.

3. Politeness = Better Role Simulation

If you’re using role prompting (e.g., “Act like a friendly tutor”), politeness naturally supports the tone you want. A polite user often gets a polite AI—like a mirror reflecting your attitude.

Final Thoughts

Prompt engineering is not magic; it's thoughtful communication. The better your input, the better the output. And with tools like ChatGPT getting smarter by the day, learning how to “talk AI” might just be the new digital literacy that makes the chatbot work even better for you.

Give these techniques a try next time you fire up ChatGPT and you might be surprised by what it can do!
